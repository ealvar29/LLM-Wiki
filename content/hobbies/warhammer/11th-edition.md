# 11th Edition — Confirmed Changes

> **Announced at AdeptiCon, March 2026. Releasing Summer 2026.**

## Launch Box

**Warhammer 40,000: Armageddon** — described as the biggest 40K launch set ever. Factions: Space Marines vs. Orks, set during Operation Imperator defending Armageddon from Ghazghkull Thraka and Wazdakka Gutsmek.

New models confirmed:
- Redesigned Intercessor Squad (Mk X armour with classic design elements)
- Updated Ork Boyz (now carry choppa, slugga, AND shoota)

**Your current codexes remain valid**, including the Armageddon: Return of Yarrick supplement content.

---

## Terrain Rules

Cover is completely reworked. Previously it gave +1 to armour saves. Now it applies **-1 to the attacker's Ballistic Skill** — a shift that makes cover much more relevant against low-AP weapons.

### The Hidden Mechanic

A brand new rule for Infantry, Beast, and Swarm units:

- Units **inside a terrain area** that **didn't shoot this turn or last turn** can be Hidden
- Hidden units can only be spotted by enemies within **15" detection range**
- Effectively eliminates first-turn alpha strikes against positioned infantry

### Terrain Structure

Terrain now has two components:
- **Terrain features** — the physical models (ruins, barricades, trees)
- **Terrain areas** — the footprint that defines where the rules apply

Most terrain is now **Obscuring**, meaning even large models and big units can use sections of terrain to block line of sight.

### Plunging Fire

Units positioned **3"+ above ground** gain **+1 to Ballistic Skill** when shooting at ground-level targets — effectively cancelling their cover bonus. TOWERING models (Imperial Knights etc.) can use this within 12" of targets.

### Objectives

Round objective markers are **gone**. Objectives are now determined by **terrain footprints** — you fight over terrain pieces, not circles on the ground. Missions include standardised balanced terrain layouts for competitive consistency.

## Combat & Charging

### Engagement Range

Increased from **1" to 2"**. Units within 2" are considered engaged. You can also now move *through* enemy engagement ranges during the Movement phase.

### Charging — Roll First, Then Pick Targets

Previously you declared targets before rolling. Now you **roll charge dice first, then select targets**. Any unit can declare a charge if there's an enemy within 12". This eliminates the brutal situation where you fail a charge and leave units in the open with no targets declared.

To complete a charge, your unit must get within the rolled distance. If models can reach **1"** of the target they must, otherwise **2"** suffices — charging around corners and into terrain is far more forgiving.

### Deep Strike Distance

Minimum deploy distance drops from **9" to 8"**, opening up more landing zones.

### Combat Disembark (returning rule)

If your transport is locked in combat, the unit inside can **charge out and immediately join the fight**.

### Pile Ins — All Before Fighting

All Pile In moves (max 3") happen **before any dice are rolled**, not interleaved with fights. The active player resolves all their Pile Ins first, then the opponent does theirs.

### Fight Order

The active player selects the **first unit to fight** — including when both players have *Fights First* units, meaning charged units can strike before the enemy's Fights First abilities.

### Overrun Fights

If a unit was engaged but its opponent is destroyed before it fights, it gets a second Pile In move toward a new eligible target.

### Consolidation

All consolidation moves happen **simultaneously** after all fighting ends, letting units reposition toward objectives or new enemies.

---

## Detachments

A significant change: you can now **combine multiple detachments** for a bespoke set of army abilities. Over **70 new and updated detachments** launch with the edition, and existing codex detachments remain valid.
